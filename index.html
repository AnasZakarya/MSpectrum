How it works

📋

Structured roadmap

Full exam guided step-by-step in the order you actually examine — history first, then cranial nerves, then body. Captures every Neurostatus item for trials, registries, and research records.

Real-time scoring

Each Functional System score and the final EDSS step update live as you fill the form — no manual table look-up.

🧠

Clinical EDSS mode

Fast adaptive scoring for neurologists in routine follow-up. Ambulation assessed first — wheelchair-bound patients done in under a minute. Uses worst-limb scoring for speed without sacrificing accuracy.

💬

Patient self-check

A plain-language adaptive questionnaire for people with MS to estimate their own EDSS. Questions adapt in real time — only the most relevant ones appear.

Print-ready Neurostatus sheet

About

AutoEDSSguide is built mainly for research applications — producing reproducible, granular EDSS scoring suitable for clinical trials, registries, and observational studies. It is also useful for routine clinical assessment.

The tool automates the EDSS calculation in real time as you fill the examination form, removing the manual look-up of conversion tables and combination rules. This saves clinicians several minutes per assessment, reduces inter-rater variability, and lets you focus on the examination itself rather than the arithmetic.

The tool implements the EDSS scoring algorithm from Kappos L., Neurostatus Scoring Definitions (2011). The structured roadmap follows our group's optimized clinical examination flow.

The Quick Check mode uses adaptive branching — questions are tailored in real time based on each answer, so only the most relevant questions are asked. This keeps the assessment brief while preserving scoring accuracy.

This tool is for research and clinical educational use. Final scoring remains the rater's clinical judgement.

    All EDSS steps (0 – 10)
    • 0Normal neurological exam; all FS = 0.
    • 1.0No disability; minimal signs in 1 FS (grade 1).
    • 1.5No disability; minimal signs in >1 FS (grade 1).
    • 2.0Minimal disability in 1 FS (grade 2, others ≤1).
    • 2.5Mild disability in 1 FS grade 2; or minimal disability in 2 FS grade 2.
    • 3.0Moderate disability in 1 FS (grade 3), or mild in 3–4 FS grade 2; fully ambulatory.
    • 3.5Fully ambulatory; moderate in 1 FS grade 3 + 1–2 FS grade 2; or 2 FS grade 3; or 5 FS grade 2.
    • 4.0Ambulatory ≥500 m without aid; severe disability in 1 FS grade 4 (or combinations).
    • 4.5Ambulatory ≥300 m without aid; relatively severe disability.
    • 5.0Ambulatory ≥200 m without aid; disability impairs full daily activities (AS = 3).
    • 5.5Ambulatory ≥100 m without aid; disability precludes full daily activities (AS = 4).
    • 6.0Requires unilateral or bilateral assistance to walk ≥100 m (AS 5–7).
    • 6.5Requires bilateral assistance to walk ≤20 m (AS 8–9).
    • 7.0Unable to walk beyond ~5 m; restricted to wheelchair; wheels self (AS = 10).
    • 7.5Unable to take a few steps; restricted to wheelchair; may need help to transfer (AS = 11).
    • 8.0Essentially restricted to bed/chair; retains some self-care; effective use of arms.
    • 8.5Essentially restricted to bed; some effective arm function; some self-care.
    • 9.0Helpless bed patient; can communicate and eat.
    • 9.5Totally helpless bed patient; unable to effectively communicate, eat, or swallow.
    • 10Death due to MS.
