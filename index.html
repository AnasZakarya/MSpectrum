MSpectrum

MSpectrum is a free, offline suite covering the spectrum of multiple sclerosis measures - the full EDSS alongside scores for cognition, fatigue, mood, walking, quality of life and disease severity, a guided patient report, and a diagnostic checker for the 2024 McDonald criteria.

Learn More ↓
🔒 Private & offline - your data never leaves your device. Please don't enter patient names or identifiers (PHI).

How it works

🧩

Comprehensive MS measures

The EDSS plus the scores that go with it - cognition (SDMT, BVMT-R, CVLT-II), fatigue (MFIS, FSS), mood (PHQ-9, GAD-7), walking (MSWS-12), quality of life (PDDS, MSQOL-54, PROMIS-29, MSIS-29) and disease severity (ARMSS, MSSS) - all in one place.

⚡

Automatic scoring

Totals, subscales, composites and the final score compute live as you enter findings - no manual conversion tables, recodes or weighting by hand.

🗣️

Results in plain language

Every score is explained in everyday words and shows where the person sits versus others - so a patient understands their own result, not just a number.

👥

For clinicians and patients

Self-report questionnaires can be completed by the patient, with full question text and written options. The full EDSS exam and performance tests are driven by the clinician.

📤

Built for research

Export structured data - every item plus the computed score - as REDCap-ready CSV or JSON, so results drop straight into trials and registries.

📄

Guided report & summaries

A guided Check my MS pathway turns a patient’s answers into one plain-language report - flagging what to discuss with their team - to print or save as PDF, alongside a print-ready Neurostatus EDSS sheet.

🔎

Diagnosis by the 2024 criteria

The MS Criteria Checker applies the 2024 McDonald criteria step by step - across relapsing, progressive and RIS onset - covering the five DIS regions, DIT, CSF and susceptibility biomarkers (CVS, PRL), with built-in mimic and older-onset safeguards.

🔒

Private & offline

Everything runs in your browser - no account, no tracking, nothing uploaded, and no patient identifiers needed. Works with or without internet after first load.

📊

Validated, normed & flagged

Every score implements a published rule, standardised into z-scores, T-scores and percentiles where norms exist (Strober, Marrie, Benedict, Parmenter, HealthMeasures, ARMSS/MSSS) with each source cited. EDSS mismatches and cross-system conflicts are flagged, and missing items handled transparently.

🧠 AutoEDSSguide

Who can use each mode. Roadmap and System view are for clinicians performing a full Neurostatus exam. Sheet Scanner processes a completed paper form automatically. Clinical EDSS is a fast 5–10 min version for routine follow-up. Quick Check is for people with MS — a plain-language self-check that estimates the EDSS without a clinical exam.
Not a diagnosis. AutoEDSSguide automates EDSS scoring from the data you enter using validated algorithms. Results require verification by a qualified neurologist before any clinical or research use.

About MSpectrum

MSpectrum is a free, offline, privacy-first hub for the outcome measures used in multiple sclerosis - the full EDSS plus cognition, fatigue, mood, walking, quality-of-life and disease-severity scores, a guided patient report, and a diagnostic checker for the 2024 McDonald criteria. Everything is computed in your browser; nothing is uploaded or stored. Built for research (reproducible, granular scoring for trials and registries) and useful for routine clinical and educational use.

🧠 AutoEDSSguide - EDSS assessment

Full Neurostatus EDSS with real-time scoring across the 7 Functional Systems + ambulation, a print-ready Neurostatus sheet, an OCR sheet scanner, inconsistency flags, and REDCap/CSV export. Includes a fast Clinical mode (streamlined sEDSS, for neurologists) and a plain-language Quick Check for people with MS. One shared, Neurostatus/Fouad-exact engine drives every view.

📊 MS Score Hub - cognition, fatigue & quality of life

Stateless calculators / questionnaires for SDMT, BVMT-R, CVLT-II, PDDS, MFIS, FSS, PHQ-9, GAD-7, MSWS-12, MSIS-29, MSQOL-54 and PROMIS-29, plus the timed motor tests T25FW and 9-HPT, PASAT-3, the BICAMS cognitive battery, the MSFC composite, and MS severity (ARMSS by age, MSSS by duration) - each with clinical interpretation, severity bands, and optional normalization (z-score & percentile). Self-report questionnaires can be filled by the patient; performance tests and composites are entered by a clinician. Nothing is stored.

⚖️ Disclaimer & terms of use

Not a diagnosis or medical advice. MSpectrum computes outcome-measure scores and applies published diagnostic criteria, and explains what they mean. It supports clinical judgement but does not itself establish a diagnosis, does not recommend, start, stop or change any treatment, and is not a substitute for examination, testing, or the judgment of a qualified clinician. Using it creates no doctor-patient relationship.

What it is for. It is intended for research, education, and to support - not replace - decisions made by patients and their care teams. Diagnosis, interpretation and treatment remain the responsibility of a qualified clinician who knows the individual.

Scores and estimates are approximations. Self-report tools (including the Quick Check EDSS estimate, PDDS and the patient questionnaires) reflect what a person reports and are not the same as a clinician-examined EDSS or a formal assessment. Norm-based results (z-scores, T-scores, percentiles) are population-level comparisons; they describe how a score relates to a reference group and do not, on their own, establish impairment in an individual.

Accuracy & verification. Scoring rules are implemented faithfully from the cited primary sources, but no guarantee is made that every result is error-free or current. Verify any score locally against the original instrument before clinical or trial use. The cognitive tests (SDMT, BVMT-R, CVLT-II) must be administered and interpreted by a trained professional.

Licensed & proprietary instruments. Some instruments are licensed or proprietary (e.g. the MSWS-12 and MSIS-29 wording, and the SDMT / BVMT-R / CVLT-II stimuli and norm tables). Their full content is not reproduced here - short functional labels are shown for orientation only. Use the official forms for clinical or research use and ensure you hold any required licence.

Mental-health screens. PHQ-9 and GAD-7 are screening tools, not diagnoses. If thoughts of self-harm are reported, or if you or someone else may be in danger, seek help immediately - in the US you can call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), or contact local emergency services.

Privacy & your responsibility. All computation is local to your browser; nothing is uploaded, stored or transmitted, and there is no account or tracking. Do not enter patient names or identifiers (PHI) - use a non-identifying code. You are responsible for using the tool in line with the privacy, data-protection and professional rules that apply to you (e.g. HIPAA, GDPR, local regulations).

No warranty. MSpectrum is provided “as is”, without warranty of any kind, express or implied. To the maximum extent permitted by law, the authors and Wayne State University accept no liability for any loss or harm arising from its use. Use is at your own discretion and risk.

How the score was derived
Final EDSS step

    All EDSS steps (0 – 10)
    • 0Normal neurological exam; all FS = 0.
    • 1.0No disability; minimal signs in 1 FS (grade 1).
    • 1.5No disability; minimal signs in >1 FS (grade 1).
    • 2.0Minimal disability in 1 FS (grade 2, others ≤1).
    • 2.5Mild disability in 1 FS grade 2; or minimal disability in 2 FS grade 2.
    • 3.0Moderate disability in 1 FS (grade 3), or mild in 3–4 FS grade 2; fully ambulatory.
    • 3.5Fully ambulatory; moderate in 1 FS grade 3 + 1–2 FS grade 2; or 2 FS grade 3; or 5 FS grade 2.
    • 4.0Ambulatory ≥500 m without aid; severe disability in 1 FS grade 4 (or combinations).
    • 4.5Ambulatory ≥300 m without aid; relatively severe disability.
    • 5.0Ambulatory ≥200 m without aid; disability impairs full daily activities (AS = 3).
    • 5.5Ambulatory ≥100 m without aid; disability precludes full daily activities (AS = 4).
    • 6.0Requires unilateral or bilateral assistance to walk ≥100 m (AS 5–7).
    • 6.5Requires bilateral assistance to walk ≤20 m (AS 8–9).
    • 7.0Unable to walk beyond ~5 m; restricted to wheelchair; wheels self (AS = 10).
    • 7.5Unable to take a few steps; restricted to wheelchair; may need help to transfer (AS = 11).
    • 8.0Essentially restricted to bed/chair; retains some self-care; effective use of arms.
    • 8.5Essentially restricted to bed; some effective arm function; some self-care.
    • 9.0Helpless bed patient; can communicate and eat.
    • 9.5Totally helpless bed patient; unable to effectively communicate, eat, or swallow.
    • 10Death due to MS.
