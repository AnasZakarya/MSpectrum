🔎 MS Criteria Checker

A questionnaire-driven calculator for the 2024 McDonald Criteria, covering onset course, MRI regions, CSF, and susceptibility biomarkers, with built-in mimic and older-onset checks.

How to use this tool
  1. Enter the patient's age and clinical onset (relapsing, progressive, or RIS).
  2. Tick which investigations were completed; the tool then asks only about those.
  3. Enter each test's findings: lesion locations, dissemination in time, and biomarkers.
  4. Review the result: the diagnosis, the criteria it rests on, and any safety cautions.
  5. Save or print the result as a PDF for the record.
Features
  • Built on the latest 2024 McDonald criteria.
  • Checks every diagnostic route, so no scenario is missed.
  • Asks only the questions that fit the tests you have.
  • Gives a clear result: the diagnosis, the reasons behind it, cautions, and next steps.
  • Works fully offline and exports to PDF.
About & methodology
This calculator implements the diagnostic logic published in Montalban X, Lebrun-Frénay C, Oh J et al. Diagnosis of multiple sclerosis: 2024 revisions of the McDonald criteria. Lancet Neurol 2025;24:850–865, together with its companion consensus papers on MRI, the visual system, and fluid biomarkers. Thresholds and pathways are taken from those sources; the full reference list appears on the results page. It is an independent educational aid, not affiliated with any commercial application.
Abbreviations
DIS: dissemination in space (lesions in ≥ 2 of 5 CNS regions)
DIT: dissemination in time (activity separated in time)
CIS: clinically isolated syndrome
RIS: radiologically isolated syndrome
PPMS: primary-progressive multiple sclerosis
OCB: CSF-restricted oligoclonal bands
k-FLC: kappa free light chain index
OCT: optical coherence tomography
GCIPL / pRNFL: retinal layers measured by OCT
VEP: visual evoked potentials
CVS: central vein sign
PRL: paramagnetic rim lesion
SWI / QSM: susceptibility MRI sequences
Gd+: gadolinium-enhancing lesion
ADEM: acute disseminated encephalomyelitis
MOGAD: MOG antibody-associated disease
NMOSD: neuromyelitis optica spectrum disorder
AQP4: aquaporin-4 antibody
LETM: longitudinally extensive transverse myelitis
Clinical disclaimer: This tool automates the 2024 McDonald calculations from the entries you provide. It supports, but does not replace, expert neurological judgement. Always exclude better explanations and mimics (MOGAD, NMOSD, vascular disease) before finalizing a diagnosis.
