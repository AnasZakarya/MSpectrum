MS Score Hub

Stateless calculators and questionnaires for the measures used alongside the EDSS - cognition, fatigue, mood, walking, quality of life and disease severity. Pick a tool, enter the data, and get the result with its plain-language meaning.

Who can use each tool. Self-report questionnaires (PDDS, MFIS, MSQOL-54, PROMIS-29, FSS, PHQ-9, GAD-7) can be filled by the patient directly - full question text and answer options are shown, and every result is explained in plain language with where the person sits versus others. Performance tests (SDMT, BVMT-R, CVLT-II, T25FW, 9-HPT) and the composites (BICAMS, MSFC) are administered by a clinician - you enter the raw results here.
Not a diagnosis. MSpectrum computes scores from validated, published instruments and explains what they mean. Interpretation, diagnosis and treatment remain the decision of a qualified clinician.
How it works. Every result updates automatically as you fill the boxes - there is no Calculate button (except the composites BICAMS and MSFC, which pull from other tools). Once you start a questionnaire, any unanswered items are highlighted in amber so you can see exactly what is missing.

SDMT

Symbol Digit Modalities Test - processing speed. Enter the number of correct substitutions in 90 seconds.

Automatic norm (US)
What is a norm? It compares this score with results from a large group of similar people - matched for age, sex and education (a process called standardisation). That turns the raw number into a z-score: about 0 = average for similar people, below 0 = below average, and z ≤ -1.5 is the impaired range. Enter the details below so the comparison can be made.
Oral: z by age/sex/education (Strober 2020). Written: z by age, 18-64 (Smith 1982 manual). No reference numbers needed.

PDDS

Patient Determined Disease Steps - the patient-reported analogue of the EDSS.

What does each step mean? (tap to collapse)
0Normal. I may have mild symptoms but they do not limit my activity; no walking limitation.
1Mild disability. Mild symptoms; no limitation in my activity.
2Moderate disability. Symptoms bother me but do not limit my activities.
3Gait disability. MS interferes with my activities, especially walking, but I can still walk without any aid.
4Early cane. I walk normally most days, but on bad days I need support (a cane, or holding the wall).
5Late cane. I need a cane or single support to walk about 25 feet; without it I cannot walk far.
6Bilateral support. I need two canes, crutches, or a walker to walk about 25 feet.
7Wheelchair / scooter. My main way of getting around is a wheelchair; I can take only a few steps.
8Bedridden. I am mostly in bed and cannot use a wheelchair for long.

MFIS

Modified Fatigue Impact Scale - 21 items, "Because of my fatigue during the past 4 weeks…" (0 = never … 4 = almost always).

MSQOL-54

Multiple Sclerosis Quality of Life-54. Enter each item's raw response (the number circled on the form). Grouped by subscale; numbers are the MSQOL-54 item order. All scores 0–100 (higher = better). Subscales use the dynamic-denominator rule (averaged over answered items only).

Labels are short reminders - refer to the MSQOL-54 form (© UCLA) for exact item wording. MSQOL-54 is free for clinical/research use.

PROMIS-29

7 domains (4 items each, 1–5) + pain intensity (0–10). Shows raw domain sums and a severity band (where the patient sits vs the general population).

FSS

Fatigue Severity Scale - 9 statements about the past week (1 = strongly disagree … 7 = strongly agree).

PHQ-9

Patient Health Questionnaire-9 - depression screen. “Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following?”

GAD-7

Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7 - anxiety screen. “Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following?”

MSWS-12

MS Walking Scale-12 - how MS has affected walking in the past 2 weeks (1 = not at all … 5 = extremely). Score 0–100, higher = greater walking limitation.

The descriptions below are our own plain-language wording (the official MSWS-12 item text is licensed, © Hobart/Thompson). Answer each on the 1–5 scale; for formal/clinical use refer to the official MSWS-12 form.

MSIS-29

MS Impact Scale-29 (v1) - the impact of MS over the past 2 weeks. Physical (items 1–20) and Psychological (items 21–29) subscales, each transformed to 0–100 (higher = greater impact).

The descriptions below are our own plain-language wording (the official MSIS-29 item text is licensed, © Hobart/Thompson). Answer each on the 1–5 scale; for formal/clinical use refer to the official MSIS-29 form.

Check my MS

Answer the questionnaires in order - press Start and you’ll be guided from one to the next. At the end you get one plain-language summary.

Please note - this is not a complete assessment. It covers only what you can report yourself. It does not include the cognitive tests (SDMT, BVMT-R, CVLT-II), which measure memory and thinking speed and must be done with a clinician - so thinking/memory is not checked here, and a normal report does not rule out cognitive changes. The full neurological EDSS also needs a clinician; you can estimate it with the Quick Check below. This is for understanding and discussion, not a diagnosis. Nothing is stored.

T25FW

Timed 25-Foot Walk - the patient walks 25 feet (7.62 m) as fast as safely possible. Enter two trials; score = average time (seconds). Lower = faster = better. Clinician-timed.

9-HPT

Nine-Hole Peg Test - place and remove 9 pegs as fast as possible; time each hand twice. Score = average time (seconds); lower = better dexterity. Clinician-timed.

BICAMS

Brief International Cognitive Assessment for MS - SDMT (processing speed), CVLT-II (verbal memory) and BVMT-R (visual memory). Enter the patient demographics once below, fill each test (buttons), and z-scores + impairment (z ≤ -1.5) compute automatically for all three.

Patient demographics (used for all three z-scores)

MSFC

Multiple Sclerosis Functional Composite - the average of three z-scores: arm (9-HPT), leg (T25FW) and cognition (PASAT-3). Standardised automatically against the NMSS Task Force reference - no reference numbers needed. Enter T25FW, 9-HPT and PASAT-3 in their tools (buttons below).

PASAT-3

Paced Auditory Serial Addition Test (3-second version) - working memory & processing speed. Enter the number of correct responses (0-60). Clinician-administered.

Clinician summary

A consolidated, printable summary of every tool you have filled in this session (raw scores + z/T + interpretation). For clinical records and research; not a diagnosis.
